Operational Constraints

This model does not:

  • Replace SDLC execution
  • Eliminate planning or documentation
  • Remove role accountability
  • Change how code is shipped
  • Define workshop formats or artifact templates
  • Require full MVP definition before production work begins

It defines:

  • How assumptions are prioritized by risk
  • When work transitions from Evidence to Commitment
  • How confidence gates control investment scale
  • How the Evidence Ledger governs progression
  • How investment scales with confidence

Glossary — Operating Model Terms

Evidence Work
The continuous practice of reducing product uncertainty through measurable signal. It governs when and how we scale build effort.
Confidence Gate
A decision point where work is evaluated based on whether uncertainty has been sufficiently reduced. Gates determine: Promote | Iterate | Pivot.
Evidence Ledger
The single source of truth for uncertainty reduction, containing: Assumptions, Signals, Decisions, and Confidence State.
Spike
A time-boxed experiment designed to test a specific assumption. Not production-grade, but instrumented to generate usable signal.
Evidence Backlog
Work focused on testing assumptions and reducing uncertainty. Time-boxed, flexible scope, not delivery commitments.
Commitment Backlog
Work focused on delivering durable capability. Scoped features, production quality, release-bound, SLA-governed.
Signal
Measurable evidence generated through experiments, user validation, or technical testing that informs assumption evaluation.
Promotion Protocol
The explicit process for transitioning work from Evidence Backlog to Commitment Backlog based on validated confidence.
Commitment State
The level of irreversible investment in a capability. Defined by architecture hardening, operational ownership, and production obligations. Commitment increases only when confidence thresholds are exceeded.
Investment Intensity
The relative level of production effort applied to a capability. Increases as confidence grows. Independent from time or phase.
Capability
A durable, production-grade behavior or system outcome derived from validated hypotheses. Not synonymous with a feature or UI element.
Hypothesis
A testable assumption about value, usability, feasibility, or viability that must be validated before commitment.
Confidence Threshold
The predefined level of validated signal required to justify increased investment or promotion to a higher commitment state.
Irreversibility
The degree to which architectural, operational, or financial decisions become difficult or costly to undo. Increases with commitment state.
